Japan’s 350 years of isolation abruptly ended in the 1850s. A timeline (Document 1) shows the events taking place in japan from 1603-1945. It shows how Japan’s isolation ended by showing the visit of U.S. Commodore Perry. “1853-54- U.S. Commodore Perry visits Japan.” This shows the beginning of foreign interaction with Japan. Japan’s Closed Country Edict of 1635 (Document 2) had banned Japanese citizens from traveling abroad. “No Japanese person is allowed to go abroad. Anyone who tries to do so will be put to death.” This is what the U.S. disrupted by forcing Japan to trade. The U.S. did so with the aforementioned Commodore Perry, who wrote a letter to the emperor of Japan (Document 3). “Therefore, as the United States and Japan are becoming every day nearer and nearer to each other, the President desires to live in peace and friendship with your imperial majesty.” This ending of Japanese isolationism would pave the way for the rapid modernization of Japan’s government.
